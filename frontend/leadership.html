LeaderShip in the Indian Army

V.K Singh

The book "Leadership in the Indian Army: Biographies of Twelve Soldiers" by Major General V.K. Singh offers an in-depth exploration of military leadership through the personal narratives of twelve distinguished Indian Army officers. Unlike conventional military biographies that often focus solely on operations or regimental histories, this work delves into the human aspects of these leaders, providing insights into their personalities, decision-making processes, and the challenges they faced.Through personal accounts, anecdotes, and reminiscences, Major General Singh sheds light on the human side of these leaders, offering a nuanced understanding of their strengths, convictions, and the complexities they navigated. The book also provides context to several historical events, including lesser-known operations like Nathu La in 1967 and Goa in 1962, and examines the role of political leaders during India's fight for independence and the partitioning of the subcontinent. This work is a valuable resource for readers interested in India's military history, leadership studies, and the personal journeys of its armed forces' stalwarts.

About the Author

Major General V.K. Singh is a distinguished retired officer of the Indian Army, renowned for his extensive service and contributions to military literature. Graduating from the National Defence Academy in May 1964 and the Indian Military Academy in June 1965, he was commissioned into the Corps of Signals. Over a 37-year career, he held several pivotal roles, including Chief Signal Officer of the Western Command and raising the signal regiment for the Army's first mechanized division in 1982. An alumnus of the Staff College, Wellington; the College of Defence Management, Secunderabad; and the National Defence College, New Delhi, Major General Singh also served as an instructor at premier military institutions. His interests span adventure sports, military history, and journalism, with contributions to various magazines and journals. Post-retirement, he has authored nine books, including Leadership in the Indian Army: Biographies of Twelve Soldiers, which offers personal accounts of exceptional military leaders, shedding light on their personalities and the human aspects of military leadership.
