Watershed 1967: India's Forgotten Victory Over China

Probal Dasgupta

If you are Buy a Book click here

"Watershed 1967: India's Forgotten Victory Over China" by Probal DasGupta is a meticulously researched account of the 1967 military clashes between India and China at Nathu La and Cho La passes along the Sikkim border. Published by Juggernaut Books in February 2020, the book sheds light on a pivotal yet often overlooked episode in India's military historyThe narrative delves into the events leading up to the 1967 confrontations, providing context through the geopolitical dynamics of the 1960s, including the aftermath of the 1962 Sino-Indian War and the 1965 India-Pakistan War. DasGupta, drawing from his experience as a former Indian Army officer and his academic pursuits, offers a detailed chronicle of the battles, highlighting strategic decisions, leadership challenges, and the valor of Indian soldiers.

About the Author

Probal DasGupta is a distinguished Indian author, former army officer, and strategic advisor. He served in the Indian Army, where he was commissioned into the elite 5th Battalion of the 11 Gorkha Rifles. His military service gave him frontline exposure to India’s defense strategy and leadership dynamics. One of his early assignments was near the Indo-China border, where he gained deep insights into border management. Probal later transitioned from military life to academia and international affairs. He earned prestigious scholarships like the JN Tata Scholarship and the Braun-Meyer Fellowship. He pursued studies in International Affairs at Columbia University, New York. His writing often reflects the blend of military experience and academic insight. He is best known for his acclaimed book "Watershed 1967: India’s Forgotten Victory Over China". The book highlights the 1967 clashes at Nathu La and Cho La, where Indian forces decisively repelled Chinese troops. It draws attention to a neglected but pivotal moment in Indian military history. The book argues that this victory rebuilt India's military morale after the 1962 war. It has been praised for its research, clarity, and powerful storytelling. The narrative focuses on General Sagat Singh and Indian soldiers' courage and resilience. The book is a significant contribution to Indian military literature. Probal also authored "Camouflaged", a story collection about leadership, mental health, and resilience.
